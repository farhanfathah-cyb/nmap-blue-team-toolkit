Learning: Scan Types & Defensive Detection

This page explains additional scan types and how defenders detect them. For safety, the toolkit may show some items as Learning-only and will not generate runnable commands for them.

Key idea

Blue teams care less about â€œhow to run itâ€ and more about:
  • What traffic patterns it creates
  • What logs/telemetry should capture it
  • How to reduce exposure and alert on abnormal behavior

Abnormal TCP Flags (NULL / XMAS concepts)

  • What it is: TCP packets with unusual flag combinations.
  • Defender signals: IDS â€œTCP flags anomalyâ€, firewall invalid-state drops, repeated probes across many ports.
  • Defenses: Stateful inspection, drop invalid packets, rate-limit and alert on sweeps.

Firewall Rule Mapping (ACK concept)

  • What it is: Probing to infer whether filtering is happening.
  • Defender signals: ACK packets without established sessions, repeated attempts across ports.
  • Defenses: Enforce state tracking, log drops, restrict exposed services, alert on scan-rate thresholds.

Practical defensive checklist

  • Keep an allowlist of approved scanner IPs and maintenance windows.
  • Enable firewall/IDS logs and centralize in SIEM.
  • Alert on port sweeps (many ports, many hosts, short time).
  • Harden exposure: close unused ports, segment networks, restrict admin ports.
  • Baseline regularly and compare drift (your History page helps).
